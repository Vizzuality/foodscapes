## Foodscapes

Distribution of terrestrial foodscapes

#### Geographic coverage
Global

#### Spatial resolution
~5 km

#### Date of content
November 2022

### Description
The production of food is at the heart of global sustainability challenges,
with unsustainable practices being a major driver of biodiversity loss, green-house gas emissions and land degradation.
The concept of foodscapes, defined as the characteristics of food production along biophysical and socio-economic gradients,
could provide an entry point into navigating this nexus.

Here we provide a globally consistent approximation of the world’s foodscape classes through the integration of best
available global data on biophysical and socio-economic factors so as to identify a minimum set of emergent clusters.

While marine and aquatic foodscapes are not within the current scope of analysis, their importance should be acknowledged.
Sustainable fisheries, aquaculture, and seaweed farming are essential components of food production.


### Methodology
Currently best available global spatial datasets on the biophysical and management properties of terrestrial
food production systems were collated, harmonized and integrated.
We then - in an unsupervised classification approach using self-organizing maps -
used those datasets to identify global clusters or archetypes of units with similar properties of food production, coined ‘foodscapes’.
We then labelled in expert-based assessment the obtained classes to identify broad classes of foodscapes that are comparable and consistent across biogeographic regions.
Different clusters were produced for biophysical and management properties, which were finally intersected to produce the global foodscape layer with a total of 86 classes.
More details on the methodology can be found in Jung et al. (2022).

## Soil groups
Distribution of recategorized USDA soil groups

#### Geographic coverage
Global

#### Spatial resolution
~5km aggregated the original 250m

#### Date of content
period 1950-2017

### Description
Predicted classes following the United States Department of Agriculture classification system. Classes shown were aggregated to greater groups of soil types.

### Methodology
Distribution of the USDA soil great groups based on machine learning predictions from global compilation of soil profiles (>350,000 training points).
To learn more about soil great groups, please refer to the
[Illustrated Guide to Soil Taxonomy - NRCS - USDA](https://www.nrcs.usda.gov/wps/PA_NRCSConsumption/download/?cid=stelprdb1247203.pdf).
Antarctica is not included.

Data made openly available by Hengl & Neumann (2018). Processing steps are outlined here
https://gitlab.com/openlandmap/global-layers/tree/master/soil

### Citation
Tomislav Hengl, & Travis Nauman. (2018). Predicted USDA soil great groups at 250 m (probabilities) (v0.2) [Data set]. Zenodo. https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.3528062
